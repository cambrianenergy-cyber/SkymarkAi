## 1. Getting Started

### The Owner’s Journey: From Invitation to Mastery
As a workspace owner in Skyymarkai, you are the operational backbone of your organization’s digital transformation. Owners are trusted with the day-to-day management of teams, agents, workflows, and sensitive data. This section will walk you through every step, from your first login to advanced workspace optimization.

#### Scenario: Your First Day as an Owner
You receive an email invitation from your founder. After clicking the link, you’re prompted to create a secure password and set up two-factor authentication. The onboarding wizard guides you through profile completion, workspace selection, and a quick tour of the dashboard. You’re introduced to your team, shown how to access the Owner’s Manual, and given a checklist to ensure nothing is missed.

### Step-by-Step: Setting Up for Success
1. **Accept Your Owner Invitation:**
	- Click the invitation link in your email. If you don’t see it, check your spam folder or contact your founder.
	- Set a strong, unique password. Use a password manager for best results.
	- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for maximum security. Skyymarkai supports authenticator apps and SMS-based 2FA.

2. **Complete Your Profile:**
	- Add your full name, contact information, and a profile photo.
	- Set your notification preferences (email, SMS, in-app alerts).
	- Review your timezone and working hours for accurate scheduling.

3. **Review Workspace Settings:**
	- Navigate to Workspace Settings from the sidebar.
	- Review data retention policies, compliance settings, and integrations.
	- Set up workspace branding (logo, color scheme) for a professional touch.

4. **Meet Your Team:**
	- View the team roster and role assignments.
	- Schedule a virtual onboarding session or team meeting.
	- Assign initial tasks or review pending agent deployments.

### Navigating the Dashboard: Your Command Center
The Skyymarkai dashboard is designed for clarity and speed. As an owner, you’ll spend most of your time here:

- **Sidebar Navigation:** Instantly access team management, agents, workflows, billing, analytics, and support.
- **Workspace Switcher:** Manage multiple workspaces or switch between them with a single click. Each workspace is isolated for security and data integrity.
- **Notifications:** Stay on top of important events, billing alerts, workflow statuses, and security updates. Customize which notifications you receive and how.
- **Help & Support:** The Owner’s Manual, support portal, and community resources are always one click away. Use the in-app chat for real-time help.

#### Pro Tips for Owners
- Bookmark the dashboard for one-click access.
- Use keyboard shortcuts to speed up navigation (see the appendix for a full list).
- Schedule a quarterly review of your workspace settings and team structure.
- Encourage your team to complete their profiles and enable 2FA.

### Key Resources for Owners
- **Support Portal:** [support.skyymarkai.com](https://support.skyymarkai.com) — Live chat, ticketing, and knowledge base.
- **API Documentation:** [docs.skyymarkai.com/api](https://docs.skyymarkai.com/api) — For custom integrations and automation.
- **Community Forum:** [community.skyymarkai.com](https://community.skyymarkai.com) — Peer support, best practices, and feature requests.
- **Owner’s Manual:** This manual is always available from the dashboard for quick reference.

## 2. Owner Privileges

### Administrative Authority: Your Command and Control
As an owner, you are the primary decision-maker for your workspace’s daily operations. You have the authority to:
- Add, remove, or reassign team members instantly, ensuring your workspace adapts to business needs.
- Assign and change roles (admin, member) to maintain a secure and efficient team structure.
- Approve or reject agent deployments, keeping automation aligned with your business strategy.
- Access and interpret workspace analytics and logs to drive data-informed decisions.

#### Scenario: Scaling Your Team
Your business lands a new client. As owner, you quickly add new team members, assign them to the right roles, and deploy agents to automate onboarding. You monitor progress in real time and adjust resources as needed.

### Billing and Subscription Management: Financial Stewardship
Owners are responsible for the financial health of the workspace. You can:
- Review invoices, payment history, and current plan details at any time.
- Update payment methods securely, ensuring uninterrupted service.
- Upgrade or downgrade subscription plans as your business evolves.
- Set up billing alerts and notifications to avoid missed payments or overages.

#### Best Practices:
- Schedule monthly billing reviews to optimize costs.
- Use the billing dashboard to forecast expenses and plan for growth.

### Security and Compliance Oversight: Protecting Your Workspace
You are the first line of defense for workspace security and compliance:
- Enforce two-factor authentication for all admins and owners.

- Ensure compliance with data protection policies (GDPR, CCPA, etc.).
- Document all security and compliance actions for audit readiness.

#### Scenario: Responding to a Security Alert
You receive a notification of a failed login attempt. As owner, you review the access logs, reset affected credentials, and notify your team. You update your incident response plan and schedule a security training session.

### Access to Confidential Documentation: Your Knowledge Advantage
Owners have exclusive access to the Owner’s Manual and other confidential resources. These materials provide:
- Advanced guidance on security, optimization, and troubleshooting.

## 3. Team & Role Management

### Building and Scaling Your Team
As an owner, you are responsible for assembling, developing, and maintaining a high-performing team. Your ability to recruit, onboard, and empower the right people is critical to your workspace’s success.

#### Step-by-Step: Adding and Onboarding Team Members
1. **Invite New Members:**
	- Use the “Invite Member” button in the Team section.
	- Enter the new member’s email and select their initial role (admin or member).
	- Add a personalized welcome message to set expectations.

2. **Onboarding:**
	- Schedule a virtual onboarding session to introduce the platform, team, and key workflows.
	- Assign a mentor or buddy for new members to accelerate learning.
	- Provide access to the Owner’s Manual and relevant documentation.

3. **Ongoing Development:**
	- Encourage continuous learning through training sessions and knowledge sharing.
	- Use performance reviews and feedback loops to support growth.

#### Scenario: Rapid Team Expansion
Your company wins a large contract and needs to double the team size. As owner, you use bulk invitations, assign roles based on project needs, and schedule a group onboarding session. You monitor progress and adjust roles as the team settles in.

### Role Definitions and Responsibilities
- **Owner:** Full administrative rights, including billing, team management, and security oversight. Owners can assign or revoke any role.
- **Admin:** Can manage agents, workflows, and templates. Admins are trusted with day-to-day operations but cannot access billing or change owner roles.
- **Member:** Can participate in workflows, use agents, and contribute to projects, but cannot modify settings or manage other users.

#### Best Practices for Role Assignment
- Assign the minimum necessary permissions to each user to reduce risk.
- Regularly review and update roles as your team evolves or projects change.
- Use bulk actions for efficient management of large teams.
- Document all role changes for compliance and transparency.

### Auditing, Compliance, and Security
Maintaining a secure and compliant team structure is essential:
- Conduct quarterly audits of team membership and roles.
- Require two-factor authentication for all admins and owners.
- Document all changes to team structure for compliance and future reference.
- Use the audit log to track invitations, removals, and role changes.

#### Scenario: Offboarding a Team Member
When a team member leaves, immediately revoke their access, reassign their tasks, and document the change. Review recent activity for security and compliance.

## 4. Agent & Workflow Administration

### Understanding Agents: The Heart of Automation
Agents are intelligent, automated entities that execute tasks, manage communications, and drive business processes. As an owner, you are responsible for deploying, configuring, and optimizing agents to maximize efficiency and value.

#### Types of Agents
- **Acquisition Agents:** Automate lead generation and outreach.
- **Content Agents:** Create, review, and publish content across platforms.
- **Engagement Agents:** Manage follow-ups, reminders, and customer interactions.
- **Analytics Agents:** Track performance, generate reports, and surface insights.

### Deploying and Configuring Agents
1. **Browse the Agent Library:**
	- Access the Agents section to view available agent types and their descriptions (see Appendix).
	- Filter by use case, department, or integration.

2. **Deploy an Agent:**
	- Click “Add Agent” and select the desired agent type.
	- Configure agent settings: name, description, permissions, and triggers.
	- Assign the agent to a workflow or project.

3. **Customize Duties and Permissions:**
	- Define what data and actions the agent can access.
	- Set up approval workflows for sensitive actions.
	- Use role-based controls to restrict agent management to owners and admins.

#### Scenario: Automating Lead Nurturing
You deploy a Lead Nurturer agent to follow up with new leads. The agent sends personalized emails, tracks engagement, and escalates hot leads to your sales team. You monitor performance and adjust messaging as needed.

### Workflow Design and Optimization
Workflows are sequences of tasks executed by agents and team members. Owners can:
- Use the visual workflow editor to design, test, and deploy complex processes.
- Assign agents to specific steps, set triggers, and define success criteria.
- Integrate external tools (e.g., CRM, email, analytics) for end-to-end automation.
- Continuously optimize workflows based on analytics and feedback.

#### Best Practices:
- Start with a clear business goal and map out each step.
- Use modular workflows for flexibility and reuse.
- Test workflows in a sandbox environment before full deployment.

### Monitoring, Troubleshooting, and Continuous Improvement
1. **Monitor Performance:**
	- Access real-time dashboards for agent and workflow metrics.
	- Set up alerts for failures, delays, or abnormal behavior.

2. **Troubleshoot Issues:**
	- Use the troubleshooting wizard to diagnose and resolve problems.
	- Review agent and workflow logs for error details.
	- Contact support for advanced troubleshooting or bug reports.

3. **Continuous Improvement:**
	- Schedule regular reviews of agent and workflow performance.
	- Gather feedback from team members and stakeholders.
	- Iterate on workflows to improve efficiency and outcomes.

### Documentation and Versioning
- Document each agent and workflow for clarity and training.
- Use version control to track changes, roll back updates, and maintain compliance.
- Store documentation in a central, accessible location for your team.

#### Scenario: Scaling Automation
Your business grows, and you need to automate new processes. You clone existing workflows, deploy additional agents, and use analytics to optimize performance. Documentation ensures new team members can quickly get up to speed.

## 5. Security & Compliance

### Data Protection: Your First Priority
As an owner, you are the guardian of your workspace’s data. Protecting sensitive information is not just a technical requirement—it’s a business imperative.

#### Key Actions for Data Protection
- **Role-Based Access Controls:** Assign the minimum permissions necessary for each user. Regularly review and update roles to prevent privilege creep.
- **Environment Variables:** Store all sensitive configuration (API keys, credentials) in environment variables, never in code or public files.
- **Access Auditing:** Use the audit log to monitor who accessed what data and when. Set up alerts for unusual access patterns.
- **Password & 2FA Enforcement:** Require strong passwords and two-factor authentication for all admins and owners. Periodically prompt users to update credentials.

#### Scenario: Preventing Data Leaks
You discover a team member has access to more data than needed. You immediately adjust their role, review recent access logs, and update your onboarding checklist to prevent future over-permissioning.

### Compliance Management: Meeting Legal and Ethical Standards
Skyymarkai provides tools to help you comply with regulations like GDPR, CCPA, and industry-specific standards.

- **Stay Informed:** Subscribe to compliance updates from Skyymarkai and relevant authorities.
- **Document Policies:** Maintain clear documentation of your data handling, retention, and deletion policies.
- **Automated Reports:** Use built-in compliance tools to generate audit trails and compliance reports for regulators or clients.
- **Regular Reviews:** Schedule annual or quarterly compliance reviews with your team and legal advisors.

#### Scenario: Responding to a Data Subject Request
A user requests deletion of their data under GDPR. You use the compliance dashboard to locate and erase all relevant records, then generate a report for your records.

### Incident Response: Be Ready for Anything
No system is immune to incidents. Owners must be prepared to respond quickly and effectively.

1. **Incident Response Plan:** Develop and document a clear plan for handling breaches, outages, or data loss.
2. **Immediate Action:** If a breach is suspected, revoke affected credentials, isolate impacted systems, and notify your team.
3. **Communication:** Inform affected users and contact Skyymarkai support for assistance.
4. **Post-Incident Review:** Analyze the incident, update your policies, and implement preventive measures.

#### Scenario: Handling a Security Breach
You receive an alert about suspicious activity. You follow your incident response plan, revoke access, notify users, and work with support to resolve the issue. Afterward, you update your security training and documentation.

### Security Best Practices: Proactive Protection
- **Rotate Secrets:** Change API keys and credentials regularly, especially after team changes or incidents.
- **Limit Data Exports:** Restrict export/download permissions to owners and trusted admins only.
- **Monitor Activity:** Set up alerts for unusual login locations, failed login attempts, or large data exports.
- **Continuous Training:** Provide regular security training for your team.

#### Pro Tip:
Use automated secret scanning (see Security Appendix) to catch accidental exposures in your codebase.

## 6. Billing & Subscription Management

### Understanding Your Subscription: Know What You’re Paying For
Owners have full visibility and control over workspace billing. Understanding your plan and usage is key to cost optimization and uninterrupted service.

#### Step-by-Step: Managing Your Subscription
1. **Review Your Plan:**
	- Access the Billing section to see your current plan tier, included features, and usage limits.
	- Compare available plans and upgrade or downgrade as needed.

2. **Access Invoice History:**
	- Download past invoices for accounting and compliance.
	- Set up automated invoice delivery to your finance team.

3. **Set Billing Alerts:**
	- Configure notifications for upcoming renewals, expiring cards, or usage overages.

#### Scenario: Scaling Up
Your business grows and you need more agents. You review plan options, upgrade instantly, and receive a prorated invoice. Billing alerts notify you of the change and next payment date.

### Managing Payments: Secure and Flexible
Owners can manage all payment methods and billing contacts:
- Add, update, or remove credit cards, ACH, or other payment options.
- Set a default payment method for recurring charges.
- Receive notifications for expiring cards or failed payments.
- Export invoices for accounting and audits.

#### Best Practices:
- Use a company card or dedicated payment method for workspace billing.
- Regularly review payment methods and remove outdated ones.

### Subscription Optimization: Get the Most Value
- Analyze usage data in the billing dashboard to select the most cost-effective plan.
- Schedule regular billing reviews to avoid overages or unused features.
- Contact support for custom billing solutions or enterprise pricing.

#### Scenario: Cost Optimization
You notice your team isn’t using all available agent slots. You downgrade your plan, saving costs without impacting productivity. The billing dashboard helps you forecast future expenses.

### Security in Billing: Protecting Your Financial Data
- Restrict billing access to owners only. Never share billing credentials.
- Use secure payment processors (Stripe) for all transactions.
- Enable two-factor authentication for billing actions.
- Monitor billing logs for unauthorized changes or suspicious activity.

#### Pro Tip:
Set up a monthly billing review with your finance team to catch issues early and optimize spend.

## 7. Integrations & API Access

### Connecting Integrations: Supercharge Your Workspace
Integrations allow you to connect Skyymarkai with your favorite tools and platforms, streamlining workflows and centralizing data.

#### Step-by-Step: Adding an Integration
1. **Open the Integrations Dashboard:**
	- Navigate to the Integrations section from the sidebar.
2. **Browse Available Integrations:**
	- Choose from a curated list (Slack, Zapier, Google Workspace, CRM tools, etc.).
3. **Follow the Setup Wizard:**
	- Authenticate with the third-party service and grant necessary permissions.
	- Configure triggers, actions, and data mappings.
4. **Test and Activate:**
	- Run a test to ensure the integration works as expected.
	- Activate and monitor ongoing activity in the integration log.

#### Scenario: Automating Notifications
You connect Slack to Skyymarkai. Now, every time a workflow completes or an agent flags an issue, your team receives an instant Slack notification. You monitor integration logs to ensure reliability.

### API Access: Unlocking Custom Automation
Skyymarkai’s API empowers owners to build custom automations, integrations, and reporting tools.

- **Generate API Keys:** In your account settings, create and manage API keys for secure access.
- **Explore the API Docs:** Review endpoints, authentication methods, and usage examples at [docs.skyymarkai.com/api](https://docs.skyymarkai.com/api).
- **Automate Everything:** Use the API to manage agents, trigger workflows, retrieve analytics, and more.
- **Rotate Keys Regularly:** For security, rotate API keys and revoke unused ones.

#### Best Practices:
- Never share API keys in public channels or code repositories.
- Use environment variables to store keys securely.
- Monitor API usage and set up alerts for unusual activity.

### Security and Monitoring: Stay in Control
- Review integration and API logs regularly for unauthorized access or errors.
- Revoke unused or compromised integrations immediately.
- Limit integration management to owners and trusted admins.

### Custom Integrations: Tailored to Your Needs
- Owners can request or build custom integrations using the Skyymarkai API.
- Contact support for integration assistance, best practices, or troubleshooting.

#### Scenario: Building a Custom CRM Sync
You use the API to sync Skyymarkai data with your in-house CRM, automating lead updates and reporting. You monitor API logs and rotate keys quarterly for security.
